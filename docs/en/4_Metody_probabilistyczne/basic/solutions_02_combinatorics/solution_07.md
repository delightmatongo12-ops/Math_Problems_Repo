# **Task 7 — k-Permutations**

### **Question**

1. First 3 places from 12 runners
2. 4-digit numbers (distinct digits 1–9)
3. Divisible by 5

---

## **Solutions**

### **1.**

$$P(12,3) = \frac{12!}{9!} = 1320$$

---

### **2.**

$$P(9,4) = 9\cdot8\cdot7\cdot6 = 3024$$

---

### **3.**

Last digit must be 5:

$$P(8,3) = 8\cdot7\cdot6 = 336$$

---

## **Teacher Questions**

* Why fix last digit? → Divisibility rule.
* Why reduce from 9 to 8? → One digit used.

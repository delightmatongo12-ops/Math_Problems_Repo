# **Task 3 — Permutations with Repeated Elements**

### **Question**

1. MISSISSIPPI
2. STATISTICS
3. STATISTICS starting with S

---

## **Solutions**

### **1.**

Counts: $M=1, I=4, S=4, P=2$

$$\frac{11!}{4!4!2!} = 34650$$

**Explanation:** Divide by repetitions.

---

### **2.**

Counts: $S=3, T=3, A=1, I=2, C=1$

$$\frac{10!}{3!3!2!} = 50400$$

---

### **3.**

Fix S → remaining counts: $S=2, T=3, A=1, I=2, C=1$

$$\frac{9!}{2!3!2!} = 7560$$

---

## **Teacher Questions**

* Why divide by factorials? → Avoid overcounting identical objects.
* Why reduce factorial in (3)? → One letter fixed.

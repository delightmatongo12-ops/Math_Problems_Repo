# **Task 10 — Urn Models**

### **Question**

1. 3 balls, no order
2. Exactly 2 red
3. Order recorded
4. Exactly 2 red (ordered)

---

## **Solutions**

Total balls: $12$

---

### **1.**

$$\binom{12}{3} = 220$$

---

### **2.**

$$\binom{5}{2}\binom{7}{1} = 10 \cdot 7 = 70$$

---

### **3.**

$$12 \cdot 11 \cdot 10 = 1320$$

---

### **4.**

Choose positions for reds and balls:

$$\binom{3}{2} \cdot 5\cdot4 \cdot 7 = 3 \cdot 20 \cdot 7 = 420$$

---

## **Teacher Questions**

* Why combination in (1)? → Order ignored.
* Why permutation in (3)? → Order matters.
* Why multiply stages? → Product rule.

# Task 6 — Probabilities for Coin Tosses

Assume a **fair coin**, meaning:

$$
P(H) = P(T) = \frac{1}{2}
$$

where

* $H$ = Heads
* $T$ = Tails

---

# 1. One Coin Toss

## Sample Space

$$
\Omega_1 = {H,T}
$$

Number of outcomes:

$$
|\Omega_1| = 2
$$

---

## Events

### Event $A_1$: Heads

$$
A_1 = {H}
$$

Probability:

$$
P(A_1) = \frac{1}{2}
$$

---

### Event $B_1$: Tails

$$
B_1 = {T}
$$

Probability:

$$
P(B_1) = \frac{1}{2}
$$

---

### Event $C_1$: Not Tails

$$
C_1 = {H}
$$

Probability:

$$
P(C_1) = \frac{1}{2}
$$

---

# 2. Two Coin Tosses

## Sample Space

$$
\Omega_2 =
{(H,H),(H,T),(T,H),(T,T)}
$$

Number of outcomes:

$$
|\Omega_2| = 4
$$

---

## Events

### Event $A_2$: Exactly One Head

Possible outcomes:

$$
A_2 = {(H,T),(T,H)}
$$

Number of favorable outcomes:

$$
|A_2| = 2
$$

Probability:

$$
P(A_2) = \frac{2}{4} = \frac{1}{2}
$$

---

### Event $B_2$: At Least One Head

The only outcome without a head is

$$
(T,T)
$$

Thus

$$
P(B_2) = 1 - \frac{1}{4}
$$

$$
P(B_2) = \frac{3}{4}
$$

---

### Event $C_2$: Same Result Twice

Possible outcomes:

$$
C_2 = {(H,H),(T,T)}
$$

Number of outcomes:

$$
|C_2| = 2
$$

Probability:

$$
P(C_2) = \frac{2}{4} = \frac{1}{2}
$$

---

# 3. Three Coin Tosses

## Sample Space Size

$$
|\Omega_3| = 2^3
$$

$$
|\Omega_3| = 8
$$

---

## Events

### Event $A_3$: Exactly Two Heads

Possible outcomes:

$$
(H,H,T),(H,T,H),(T,H,H)
$$

Number of outcomes:

$$
|A_3| = 3
$$

Probability:

$$
P(A_3) = \frac{3}{8}
$$

---

### Event $B_3$: At Least One Tail

Only one outcome has **no tails**:

$$
(H,H,H)
$$

Thus

$$
P(B_3) = 1 - \frac{1}{8}
$$

$$
P(B_3) = \frac{7}{8}
$$

---

### Event $C_3$: All Tosses Equal

Possible outcomes:

$$
(H,H,H),(T,T,T)
$$

Number of outcomes:

$$
|C_3| = 2
$$

Probability:

$$
P(C_3) = \frac{2}{8}
$$

$$
P(C_3) = \frac{1}{4}
$$

---

# Possible Questions and Answers

---

## Question 1

**What is the probability of exactly one head in two tosses?**

Answer:

$$
P = \frac{2}{4} = \frac{1}{2}
$$

---

## Question 2

**What is the probability of at least one head in two tosses?**

Answer:

$$
P = 1 - \frac{1}{4} = \frac{3}{4}
$$

---

## Question 3

**What is the probability of exactly two heads in three tosses?**

Answer:

$$
P = \frac{3}{8}
$$

---

## Question 4

**What is the probability that all three tosses give the same result?**

Answer:

$$
P = \frac{2}{8} = \frac{1}{4}
$$

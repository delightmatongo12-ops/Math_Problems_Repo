# Task 4 — Weekly Weather

Suppose the weather for each day can be one of three states:

* $S$ = Sunny
* $C$ = Cloudy
* $R$ = Rainy

Thus the set of possible outcomes for **one day** is

$$
\Omega = {S, C, R}
$$

---

# 1. Sample Space for One Day

The possible outcomes are

$$
\Omega_1 = {S, C, R}
$$

Number of outcomes:

$$
|\Omega_1| = 3
$$

Each elementary outcome represents the **weather on that day**.

Example outcomes:

* $S$
* $C$
* $R$

---

# 2. Sample Space for Two Days

For two days, outcomes are **ordered pairs**.

$$
\Omega_2 = \Omega \times \Omega
$$

Possible outcomes:

$$
\Omega_2 =
{(S,S),(S,C),(S,R),(C,S),(C,C),(C,R),(R,S),(R,C),(R,R)}
$$

Number of outcomes:

$$
|\Omega_2| = 3^2 = 9
$$

Example outcome:

$$
(S,R)
$$

means

* Day 1 = Sunny
* Day 2 = Rainy

---

# 3. Sample Space for Seven Days (One Week)

For a full week:

$$
\Omega_7 = \Omega^7
$$

Number of possible weather sequences:

$$
|\Omega_7| = 3^7
$$

$$
|\Omega_7| = 2187
$$

---

# 4. Meaning of an Elementary Outcome

An elementary outcome describes the **weather for each day of the week**.

Example:

$$
(S,C,R,S,S,C,R)
$$

Meaning:

* Monday = Sunny
* Tuesday = Cloudy
* Wednesday = Rainy
* Thursday = Sunny
* Friday = Sunny
* Saturday = Cloudy
* Sunday = Rainy

---

# Possible Questions and Answers

---

## Question 1

**What is the sample space for one day of weather?**

Answer:

$$
\Omega = {S,C,R}
$$

---

## Question 2

**How many possible outcomes exist for two days of weather?**

Answer:

$$
|\Omega_2| = 3^2 = 9
$$

---

## Question 3

**How many possible weather sequences exist for one week?**

Answer:

$$
|\Omega_7| = 3^7
$$

$$
|\Omega_7| = 2187
$$

---

## Question 4

**What does the outcome**

$$
(R,S,C,S,R,C,S)
$$

**represent?**

Answer:

It describes the **weather for each day of the week**, in order.

---

## Question 5

**Why is the number of outcomes equal to $3^7$?**

Answer:

Because each of the **7 days** has **3 possible weather states**, so

$$
3 \times 3 \times 3 \times 3 \times 3 \times 3 \times 3 = 3^7
$$

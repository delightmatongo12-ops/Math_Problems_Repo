# Task 5 — Buffon's Needle Experiment

Buffon’s Needle is a **geometric probability experiment** where a needle is dropped onto a floor with **parallel lines** spaced a distance $d$ apart.

To describe the outcome of the experiment, we need two variables.

---

# 1. Variables Describing the Outcome

Each needle drop can be described by:

* $X$ = distance from the **center of the needle** to the **nearest line**
* $\theta$ = angle between the **needle and the parallel lines**

---

# 2. Possible Values of the Variables

The distance $X$ can range from

$$
0 \le X \le \frac{d}{2}
$$

because the center of the needle cannot be farther than **half the distance between two lines**.

The angle $\theta$ can range from

$$
0 \le \theta \le \frac{\pi}{2}
$$

because angles larger than $\frac{\pi}{2}$ produce the **same configuration** due to symmetry.

---

# 3. Sample Space

The sample space consists of **all possible pairs** $(X,\theta)$.

Thus the sample space is

$$
\Omega = \left[0,\frac{d}{2}\right] \times \left[0,\frac{\pi}{2}\right]
$$

---

# 4. Type of Sample Space

This sample space is **continuous**, because both $X$ and $\theta$ can take **infinitely many real values**.

Thus

$$
|\Omega| = \infty
$$

---

# 5. Meaning of an Elementary Outcome

An elementary outcome is a pair

$$
(X,\theta)
$$

Example:

$$
\left(\frac{d}{4},\frac{\pi}{6}\right)
$$

This means:

* the needle center is $\frac{d}{4}$ units from the nearest line
* the needle forms an angle of $\frac{\pi}{6}$ with the lines
